## Figure 1: The Σ-Model Conceptual Framework
**Label:** `fig:sigma-framework` | **Type:** TikZ

A schematic diagram of the Σ-Model knowledge representation. Two vertical pillars stand on a shared horizontal base. The base represents functional breadth β_A (shared foundational knowledge across domains), filled with a crosshatch pattern. The left pillar (domain d₁) is taller than the right (domain d₂), representing parametric depth δ_A(d). Both pillars are overlaid with diagonal-line hatching indicating schema coherence σ_A. A horizontal bridge connects the pillars at the threshold height θ_I, labelled Ψ_A (inactive), representing the intersection activation condition — cross-domain discovery is only possible when both domains exceed the activation threshold. A dashed line at the top marks the domain frontier Δ(t), the theoretical maximum depth. A dotted red line at mid-height marks the activation threshold θ_I. The y-axis reads "Normalised Value [0, 1]" and the x-axis reads "Domain". A legend in the upper right explains all visual elements.

## Figure 2: The Five-Phase Training Arc
**Label:** `fig:phases` | **Type:** PGFPlots/TikZ

A time-series plot with Training Time (t) on the x-axis (unmarked ticks) and Normalised Value [0, 1] on the y-axis, with key ticks at 0, σ_critical (0.3), 0.65, and 1. Three curves are plotted: (1) δ_A^relative (solid black, logistic curve) — rises gradually from near 0, crosses 0.5 around mid-training, approaches 1; (2) α_A (dotted green) — rises sharply early then plateaus near 0.85; (3) σ_A (solid blue) — remains near 0 through Phases 0–1, then crosses σ_critical at a red star marker at the Phase 1→2 boundary, rises sharply to approach 1. Background shading alternates between white and light grey to delineate six phases: P0 (grey, initialisation), P1 (white, depth accumulation), P2 (blue, crystallisation — the σ_A bifurcation), P3 (white, frontier navigation), P4 (grey, multi-domain), P5 (white, expansion). A phase bar below the plot labels each phase with a brief description in footnotesize font (≥8pt).

## Figure 3: Coupled Dynamical System Architecture
**Label:** `fig:coupled-system` | **Type:** TikZ

A two-panel node-and-edge diagram. Panel (a) "Core Coupling Loop" (left, grey border) contains four nodes: Parametric Depth δ_A (solid box, blue fill with diagonal hatching), Schema Coherence σ_A (dashed box, green fill with dot pattern), Attentional Fidelity α_A (solid box, orange fill with crosshatch), and Training Signal C_A (dashed grey box). Edges: C_A → α_A ("drives"), α_A → σ_A ("gates"), σ_A ⊣ δ_A ("reduces decay", bar-headed), δ_A → α_A ("supports", curved). Panel (b) "Metacognitive Extension" (right, grey border) contains three nodes: σ_A (dashed, green/dots), Self-Model M̂_A (dashed, purple/horizontal lines), and Shortcut Learning Ω_SL (solid, red fill with vertical lines). Edges: σ_A → M̂_A ("calibrates"), Ω_SL ⊣ M̂_A ("inflates"), Ω_SL ⊣ σ_A ("suppresses"). A legend below explains: solid border = observable, dashed = latent, arrow = promotes/gates, bar-head = suppresses.

## Figure 4: The Compositional Generalisation Failure Mode
**Label:** `fig:diagnostic` | **Type:** TikZ

A 2×2 quadrant diagram with Parametric Depth δ_A on the x-axis (Low → High) and Schema Coherence σ_A on the y-axis (Low → High). Four quadrants: top-left (white) = Moderate ID / Moderate OOD; top-right (green fill) = "Target State" with High ID and High OOD, marked with a green star; bottom-left (grey) = Low ID / Low OOD; bottom-right (red fill with grid pattern) = "Illusion of Mastery" — High In-Distribution Accuracy but Fails OOD Recombination, annotated as "(Invisible to standard benchmarks)" and marked with a black square. A dashed black arrow curves from the top-left quadrant down to the bottom-right, labelled "Standard SGD", illustrating that conventional training without schema-coherence coupling drives agents toward the Illusion of Mastery rather than the Target State.

## Figure 5: Main Experimental Results
**Label:** `fig:sigma-main-results` | **Type:** PNG (regenerated by `scripts/generate_figures.py`)

A 2×2 panel figure. (A) Top-left: "In-Distribution Learning Curves" — line plot of ID accuracy (%) vs training step for three conditions (Baseline=blue, Additive=orange, Multiplicative=green), each with ±1σ shaded error band. All conditions converge to ~90–98% ID accuracy by step 2000. (B) Top-right: "Final OOD Accuracy by Condition" — vertical bar chart with error bars. Baseline reaches 44.5%, Additive 97.0%, Multiplicative 94.1%. (C) Bottom-left: "Schema Coherence Trajectories" — line plot of σ̃_A vs training step. Baseline rises slowly to ~0.55; Additive plateaus near 0.35; Multiplicative rises steeply to ~0.85. Horizontal dashed red line at σ_critical=0.15; dotted purple line at δ*=0.55. All curves include ±1σ error bands. (D) Bottom-right: "Phase Distribution" — horizontal stacked bar chart showing percentage of training steps in each phase across Σ-Model conditions: P2 Crystallise (44.1%), P3 Intersection (42.5%), P0 Pre-Init (13.4%). Colours are Okabe-Ito colour-blind-safe.

## Figure 6: Prediction 6 — Multiplicative vs Additive Model Comparison
**Label:** `fig:prediction-6-comparison` | **Type:** PNG (regenerated by `scripts/generate_figures.py`)

Two-panel figure. (A) "Model Comparison": scatter plot of observed cross-domain discovery Ψ_A (grey dots) vs schema coherence σ̂_A, with two fitted curves — multiplicative model (solid green, R²=0.616) and additive model (dashed orange, R²=0.167). A light green 95% confidence interval band surrounds the multiplicative fit. The multiplicative curve fits the data substantially better. (B) "Residual Analysis": scatter plot of residual error vs σ̂_A for both models. Multiplicative residuals (green circles) are randomly scattered around zero with no systematic pattern. Additive residuals (orange crosses) show a clear funnel/heteroscedastic pattern, indicating model misspecification. X-axis is labelled "Schema Coherence σ̂_A" (corrected from previous "σ_A Intensity").

## Figure 7: Prediction 9 — Phase 2 Entry Inflection Point
**Label:** `fig:prediction-9-inflection` | **Type:** PNG (regenerated by `scripts/generate_figures.py`)

A single-panel scatter plot of mean OOD accuracy (%) vs training timesteps (0–500) for Σ-Model conditions. Light blue scatter points show raw OOD accuracy at each eval step. A thick dark red segmented regression line fits two linear regimes: pre-inflection slope β₀=0.0124 (shallow rise) and post-inflection slope β₁=0.0462 (steeper rise). The inflection point τ̂=300 is marked by a vertical dotted black line, with an arrow annotation. A light red shaded band (95% CI on τ̂) spans roughly steps 240–360. A light red 95% CI band on the regression line runs along the full trajectory. The figure demonstrates the predicted Phase 2 entry inflection — OOD accuracy accelerates sharply after schema coherence crosses σ_critical.
